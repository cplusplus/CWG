2989. Remove misleading general allowance for parentheses

Section: 7.5.4  [expr.prim.paren]     Status: open     Submitter: Brian Bi     Date: 2025-01-17

(From submission #661.)

The second sentence in 7.5.4 [expr.prim.paren] paragraph 1 occasionally causes confusion whether parenthesized constructs are allowed where the grammar or other rules do not specifically allow them.

Possible resolution:

Change 7.5.4 [expr.prim.paren] paragraph 1 as follows:

A parenthesized expression (E) is a primary expression whose type, result, and value category are identical to those of E. The parenthesized expression is a bit-field if E is a bit-field. The parenthesized expression can be used in exactly the same contexts as those where E can be used, and with the same meaning, except as otherwise indicated.
